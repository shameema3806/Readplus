You've relocated to the UAE. Your child's school application is submitted. And now you wait.

For many Indian expat families in Al Ain, this waiting period stretches from 3 to 6 months — sometimes longer. Every day of inactivity risks falling behind academically.

Here's what you should do.

## Understand the Risk of the Gap

Children learn by continuity. When learning stops abruptly — even for a few months — several things happen:

- **Skills regress.** Reading fluency, mental math speed, and writing habits all decline without daily practice.
- **Confidence drops.** When a child finally joins school and finds classmates ahead, it creates self-doubt that can take months to recover from.
- **Study habits fade.** A structured routine is hard to rebuild from scratch.

This isn't a reason to panic. It's a reason to plan.

## Option 2: Structured Home Learning

If a training institute isn't immediately feasible, structured home learning can help — but it requires discipline.

What you'll need:
- NCERT textbooks for the relevant grade (available online or from Indian stores)
- A fixed daily schedule (minimum 3–4 hours of focused learning)
- Workbooks for practice (Maths, English, EVS/Science)
- Regular reading habit — 30 minutes daily

**The challenge:** Most parents aren't trained teachers, and children rarely take home instruction as seriously as classroom instruction.

## Option 3: Online Tutoring

Several platforms offer online CBSE tutoring. This can supplement home learning but shouldn't replace it entirely for young children (Grades 1–4), who benefit far more from face-to-face, structured interaction.

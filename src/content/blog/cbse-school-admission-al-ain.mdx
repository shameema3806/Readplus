Relocating to Al Ain with children is an exciting chapter — but securing a school seat can be one of the most stressful parts. This guide walks Indian expat parents through everything you need to know about CBSE school admission in Al Ain.

## How Long Does Admission Take?

School admission in Al Ain can take anywhere from **3 to 9 months**, depending on:

- The school's waiting list at the time you apply
- Your visa and Emirates ID processing timeline
- Availability of seats at the appropriate grade level
- Transfer certificate authentication from your home country

Many families arrive in Al Ain months before their child secures a seat. This gap — if not managed — can set a child back significantly.

## Documents Required for CBSE School Admission

To apply at most CBSE schools in Al Ain, you will typically need:

1. **Original Transfer Certificate (TC)** — attested by your home state's Education Department and the UAE Embassy
2. **Academic records** from the previous school (Report Cards / Progress Reports)
3. **Child's passport** (valid) and UAE visa page
4. **Child's Emirates ID** (or proof of application)
5. **Parent's Emirates ID** and residence visa
6. **Birth certificate** (attested)
7. **2–4 passport-size photographs**

> Pro tip: Start the TC attestation process *before* you leave India. It can take 2–4 weeks in India, and some schools won't even process your application without it.

## Top CBSE Schools in Al Ain

Several CBSE-affiliated Indian schools operate in Al Ain. Most follow the NCERT curriculum and have competitive admission processes.

When applying, ask specifically about:
- Grade-level seat availability
- Estimated timeline from application to seat allocation
- Whether they accept students mid-year
